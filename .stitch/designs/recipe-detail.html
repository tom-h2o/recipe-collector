Herb-Crusted Rack of Lamb

A celebration of seasonal aromatics. Tender, grass-fed lamb enveloped in a vibrant crust of mint, rosemary, and sourdough crumbs, roasted to a delicate pink finish.

Ingredients

AI Scaler 2 Servings
  • Rack of Lamb (8 ribs) 1 lb
  • Fresh Rosemary, minced 2 tbsp
  • Fresh Mint, chopped 1/4 cup
  • Sourdough Breadcrumbs 1/2 cup
  • Dijon Mustard 3 tbsp
  • Garlic Cloves, crushed 3 pcs
  • Sea Salt & Black Pepper to taste

Nutrition Facts

Calories 480 kcal
Protein 34g
Fat 28g
Carbs 12g
Keto Friendly High Protein

The Preparation

1

Preparation & Searing

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Season the lamb racks generously with sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper. Heat a heavy skillet over high heat with a splash of olive oil and sear the lamb on all sides until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.

2

Herb Crust Assembly

In a small bowl, combine the sourdough breadcrumbs, minced rosemary, chopped mint, and crushed garlic. Stir in a teaspoon of olive oil to moisten. Brush the fatty side of the lamb generously with Dijon mustard—this acts as the glue for your aromatics.

3

Roasting

Press the herb mixture firmly onto the mustard-coated lamb. Place the racks in a roasting pan, bone-side down. Roast for 15-20 minutes for medium-rare, or until the internal temperature reaches 130°F (54°C).

4

Resting & Carving

Transfer the lamb to a warm plate and tent loosely with foil. Allow it to rest for at least 10 minutes. This ensures the juices redistribute, resulting in a tender texture. Carve between the ribs and serve with a sprig of fresh mint.

eco
Chef's Notes

"The secret to a crust that stays on is the quality of your mustard and the fineness of the breadcrumbs. If using store-bought crumbs, pulse them in a processor for 10 seconds with the fresh herbs for a more vibrant, integrated flavor."
